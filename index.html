Remember your dreams while you dream them

Build a gentle lucid dreaming routine.

Lucid dreaming is the experience of realizing that you are dreaming while the dream is still happening. With practice, that awareness can help you explore dreams more intentionally, rehearse skills or creative ideas, notice recurring dream signs, and wake with clearer dream memories.

This toolkit keeps your practice local to your browser: record dreams, set touch-friendly timers, rehearse MILD and SSILD, and train a soft audio cue for targeted lucidity reactivation.

Suggested daily flow

  1. Morning: write down every remembered fragment in the dream journal.
  2. Daytime: optionally enable gentle reality-check prompts.
  3. Bedtime: train your cue, schedule WBTB or TLR playback, and sleep.
  4. Night waking: practice MILD or SSILD before returning to sleep.

Educational tool only. Use lucid-dream alarms cautiously and prioritize healthy sleep.
